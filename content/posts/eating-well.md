## Intro

There are main considerations when thinking about food and diet:

- Flavour and culinary culture
- Nutrition and health
- Economic and social impact of food production
- Ecological impact of food production and global justice
- Animal suffering

## Flavour and culinary culture

Personally, I really enjoy cooking and experimenting with new ingredients, flavours and recipes.
I also appreciate culinary culture and the social experiences associated with preparing and sharing food.
But compared with others, I seem to be less swayed by flavour.
One of my favourite foods is porridge, just oats and water, maybe with some fresh pears and pear jam, or blueberries when available.

### Vitamin B12

2.4 mcg per day

Prefer hydroxo- and/or methyl-B12 (instead of cyanocobalamin)
Prefer capsules over tablets.

Absorption: 1.5mcg (intrinsic factor) + 1% of dose
500mcg capsule = 1.5mcg + 5mcg = 6.5mcg : good for 2 days

naturally it occurs exclusively in animal products, eggs (low bioavailability) and dairy products; synthetically in fortified foods (breakfast cereals, fruit juices, etc.); Vitamin B12 is not contained in plant products, but is rather a byproduct of microorganisms that live on plants and in the soil

http://www.vitaminb12.de/
http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/VitaminB12-HealthProfessional/
http://www.veganhealth.org/b12/rec
http://www.vegane-gesellschaft.org/b12-tipps

### Calcium

700-1000mg per day, upper limit 2500mg per day for adults (19-50 years)

in blackstrap molasses (syrup from raw sugar cane)
in flaxseeds (234mg/100g), sesame seeds (975mg/100g), broccoli (40mg/100g), spinach (136mg/100g), marrowstem kale (field cabbage), bok choy (Chinese cabbage), curly kale (72mg/100g), poppy seed (1400mg/100g), soy products and okra
in fortified foods (soy milk 120mg/100g)
in dairy products (whole milk 113mg/100g)

### Vitamin D

5-25mcg/1000 UI per day, safe up to 2000 UI per day

especially from autumn to spring
in capsules instead of tablets
take with meal
http://www.hsph.harvard.edu/nutritionsource/vitamin-d/#vitamin-d-deficiency-a-global-concern

### Zinc

11mg per day, safe up to 40mg per day
inhibited intake in vegetarians, may need up to 50% more

in oats (3.64mg/100g), sunflower seeds (5mg/100g), sesame seeds (7.75mg/100g)
http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Zinc-HealthProfessional/#h8

### Iron

8-10mg per day

in grains, legumes, dried fruits (peach, apricot), seeds and nuts (pumpkin seeds, sesame (14.55mg/100g), sunflower seeds, flaxseeds), vegetables (spinach, parsley) and fortified foods

bioavailability for the body in plant-based foods is lower
iron absorption is increased with the simultaneous intake of promoting substances (Vitamin C, organic acids), and inhibited with the simultaneous intake of black tea, coffee or dairy products
is not destroyed during heating

### Proteins

0.8 - 2g per 1kg body weight per day, so 64g-160g for 80kg body weight per day

essential amino acids completely in hemp seeds, quinoa and in eggs and dairy products

in soy products (tofu, tempeh)
among others in legumes (lentils, beans, chickpeas), grains

https://www.dge.de/presse/meldungen/2020/positionspapier-zur-proteinzufuhr-im-sport/

Essential Fatty Acids
in hemp seeds, walnuts, flaxseeds (flaxseeds/ linseeds)

### Iodine

150mcg daily
in iodised salt
http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Iodine-HealthProfessional/

### Selenium

55mcg daily, no more than 400mcg

brazil nuts (1900mcg/100g), sunflower seeds (78mcg/100g), sesame seed (34mcg/100g), cashew nuts (19mcg/100g)

selenium content in plant-based foods varies greatly depending on soil
http://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Selenium-HealthProfessional/
